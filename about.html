Life is dream was first published in 1636.
Calderón de la Barca, its author, was obessed with
a concept of life as theatre, shadows and dreams.
In this play, Segismundo, a prince imprisoned since birth
in a tower, struggles between the world of dreams,
appearances and reality.
A curse had him imprisoned for life
but unlike in a tragedy he is able
to change his destiny.
The play is divided in three acts.
In these pages you can read both the spanish
and English versions of Segismundo's monologues.
